Literature

Homer: The Iliad and The Odyssey

The Iliad: Rodney Merrill's translation of the Iliad (University of Michigan Press, 2009) translates Homer's original Greek into English hexameters. It comes as close to matching the sound and the style of the original poem as is possible in English. A recent translation by Peter Greene is also outstanding. It is almost like hexameter, but not as rigid as Merrill.

The Odyssey: Like his translation of the Iliad, Rodney Merrill's translation of the The Odyssey (University of Michigan Press, 2002) strictly adheres to dactylic hexameter, the meter of Homeric poetry. Allen Mandelbaum's translation is also excellent. It is free verse, but more conservative than Fagles.

Additional material: The Iliad, or The Poem of Force by Simone Weil (1939) is a brilliant and influential essay about the Iliad containing reflections on the conclusions one can draw from the epic regarding the nature of force in human affairs.

Plato

For Plato's complete works, I recommend the edition by John Cooper (Hackett Publishing Co., 1997).

For Symposium, a dialogue on the nature of love, perhaps the most beautiful book ever written, R. E. Allen's translation (Yale University Press, 1993) is really good.

Plutarch

I recommend the Dryden Edition, Volume 1 and Volume 2 (Modern Library Classics, 2001).

Bible

The New Oxford Annotated Bible with the Apocrypha, Revised Standard Version, Expanded Edition.
Edited by Bruce M. Metzger and Herbert G. May. Oxford University Press, Revised Standard edition, 1977.

Shakespeare

The Royal Shakespeare Company's edition (Modern Library, 2010) of the complete Shakespeare is an excellent modern edition.

Mathematics

Euclid: The Elements

Get Sir Thomas Heath's three volume translation published by Dover Publications: Volume 1 (Books 1-2), Volume 2 (Books 3-9), and Volume 3 (Books 10-13). Everyone must at least work through Book 1.

George Chrystal: Algebra

George Chrystal's Algebra - An Elementary Textbook was recently reprinted by the American Mathematical Society in two beautifully bound hardcover volumes. Sorry for the inconsistent pictures; the actual books are identical in color.

Programming

Knuth: The Art of Computer Programming

 

Knuth's The Art of Computer Programming, particularly volumes 1, 2, and 3, are incomparable to anyting else in our field.

Fascicle 1 - MMIX updates Knuth's original assembly language to reflect later developments in computer architecture.

Tarjan: Data Structures and Network Algorithms

Bob Tarjan's Data Structures and Network Algorithms is the best book about combinatorial and graph algorithms.

He should have written more books.

Hennessy and Patterson: Computer Architecture

Two textbooks by John Hennessy and David Patterson, Computer Organization and Design (introductory textbook), and Computer Architecture, A Quantitative Approach (graduate textbook) revolutionized the teaching and practice of computer architecture by training an entire generation to think systematically and quantitatively about computer architecture.

Music

There are many good recordings of the pieces I recommend. I have chosen my personal favourites, subject to the constraint that they should be in print at the time of writing (Oct 2015).

Johann Sebastian Bach

The Well-Tempered Clavier, performed by Glenn Gould

St. Matthew's Passion, New York Philharmonic Orchestra, conducted by Leonard Bernstein

Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart

The Marriage of Figaro, Philharmonia Orchestra and Chorus, Elisabeth Schwarzkopf

Don Giovanni, New Philharmonia Orchestra and Chorus, Nilolai Gedda

Ludwig van Beethoven

The Nine Symphonies, Berliner Philharmoniker, conducted by Herbert von Karajan

The String Quartets, Emerson String Quartet

Sonatas, Emil Gilels

Franz Schubert

Lieder, Elisabeth Schwarzkopf

The Song Cycles, Dietrich Fischer-Dieskau

Richard Strauss

Der Rosenkavalier (Film), Vienna Philharmonic Orchestra, Elisabeth Schwarzkopf, Herbert von Karajan, and others.

Art

Civilization, A Personal View (TV Series) is a highly acclaimed documentary series about the history of western art, architecture, and philosophy. Written and presented by the British art historian Kenneth Clark (later Lord Clark), it is, by far, the best and easiest introduction to western art.
